# Technical Design: Noir-ShadowWire Binding (Nullifier-as-Nonce)

## Objective
Establish a cryptographic link between the Agent's Identity (Noir) and the Financial Transaction (ShadowWire) to prevent "Proof Replay" and "Transaction Decoupling" attacks.

## The Concept
Use the `nullifier` generated by the Noir circuit as the source of truth for the `nonce` in the ShadowWire transfer.

## Data Mapping & Transformation
Since Noir nullifiers are 254-bit fields and ShadowWire nonces are typically 64-bit integers (u64):

1. **Agent Side (SDK):**
   - Generate Noir Proof -> Extract `nullifier` (32 bytes).
   - Derive `sw_nonce` = `Keccak256(nullifier)[0..8]` (First 8 bytes as a Little Endian u64).
   - Call `shadowwire.uploadProof(nonce: sw_nonce, ...)`.

2. **On-Chain Side (Gateway):**
   - Receive `ProofPayload` (containing the 32-byte Noir nullifier).
   - Receive `sw_proof_pda` (ShadowWire's commitment account).
   - **Validation Step 1:** Verify Noir proof and record/check the 32-byte nullifier PDA.
   - **Validation Step 2:** 
     - Calculate `derived_nonce` = `Keccak256(noir_nullifier)[0..8]`.
     - Load `sw_proof_pda` data.
     - Assert `sw_proof_pda.nonce == derived_nonce`.

## Success Metrics
- **Atomicity:** A ShadowWire payment cannot be reused for a different Noir identity proof.
- **Double-Spending Prevention:** The Noir nullifier PDA prevents the same identity from acting twice, while ShadowWire's internal logic prevents the money from being spent twice.
- **Auditor Visibility:** An auditor can verify the link without knowing the Agent's secret.
